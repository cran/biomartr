Introduction to biomartr

2015-08-07

Getting Started

A major problem in bioinformatics research is consistent data retrieval. The biomartr package therefore, aims to provide users with easy to use and diverse interfaces to well curated biological databases such as:

  • NCBI refseq
  • BioMart
  • Gene Ontology

The collection of functions implemented in biomartr enable fast functional annotation queries for a set of genes or entire genomes such as biological pathway analyses and biological sequence retrieval.

Users of the myTAI and orthologr packages will find additional functions allowing them to integrate biomartr to any workflow implemented using myTAI or orthologr. The biomartr package is designed to achieve the highest degree of reproducible phylotranscriptomics research from data retrieval to data visualization.

Workflow Design

The bopmartr package is designed to cover the following branches of biological data retrieval:

  • Biological Sequence Retrieval
  • Functional Annotation Retrieval
  • Enrichment Analyses

Hence, all functions implemented in biomartr serve to fulfill the above listed tasks.

Biological Sequence Retrieval

In the post-genomic era, biological sequences are used to investigate most phenomena of molecular biology. The growing number of databases and their entries allows us to design meta-studies in a new dimension and to re-investigate known phenomena from a new perspective. Neverthless, from a data science point of view this vast amount of heterogenous data, coming from very different data resources and data standards is very hard to transform from heterogenous to homogenous data. The detection of significant patterns within meta-analyses, therefore relies on high quality data analysis and data science.

Another aspect is reproducibility. Even in cases where a high degree of data homogeneity is achieved, the aspect of scientific reproducibility adds up to a new layer of complexity. Much effort is now being invested to enable high standards of reproducibility in data driven sciences (e.g. ROpenSci). The biomartr package aims to be a part of this data science movement. The Sequence Retrieval vignette will introduce users to the process of biological sequence retrieval using biomartr. All functions were designed to allow users to achieve the highest (yet possible) degree of reproducibility and transparency for their own analyses.

Functional Annotation Retrieval

Most phenomena in molecular biology are based on the interaction of genes and their products (proteins). So in case patterns can be found by investigating biological sequences, in most cases it is of interest to map this pattern to a biological function. The Gene Ontology and Kegg consortia provide extensive information on gene functions and pathway memberships.

The biomartr packages provides powerful interfaces to functional annotation for specific genes. Here, users can consult the Functional Annotation vignette which introduces them to the process of functional information retrieval.

Enrichment Analyses

Successfully mapping patterns to functions is the first analytical step in biological research. The next step is to statistically quantify the significance of the observed pattern. Enrichment Analyses statistically quantify the significance of gene functions that correspond to an observed pattern.

The biomartr package provides useful interfaces to gene enrichment analyses to enable the statistical validation of oberved patterns.
